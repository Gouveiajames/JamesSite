Discoveries & Insights

I chose to use area graphs as they are a good way of showing changes in the number of items. An area graph shows at a glance the general shape and rate of growth/contraction of each measurement. This graph appears to show a general inverse relationship between inflation and money supply but this is misleading. I was really surprised that there did not seem to be a direct connection between money supply and inflation. It seems most of the effects of money creation seems to be in hard assets such as home prices. Inflation is far more complicated than just money supply in circulation. I think taking a look at how the US dollar is used in international trade may shed light on what this graph is showing.

As can be seen by the size of the square for the U.S., the dollar is used in most worldwide transactions. This is due to many commodities priced in the dollar as well as the dollar being a tier 1 asset, considered the most stable in the world. Since the dollar is traded so heavily this has allowed the US to borrow well beyond its means and still not face a lot of inflation. Other counties, do not have this advantage and thus face a lot more inflation.

The trends of Crude Oil Prices and the Angolan GDP by Year. Angola is a very resource rich country in Africa which makes it vulnerable to the "resource curse" which happens when an economy becomes too dependent on resources to drive economic growth. In developed and developing economies (such as America), several sectors lead to economic growth making them more resilient to resource price changes.

The trends of construction and hospitality jobs by Year. Developed countries in the world have seen a shift from labor jobs to a service economy. This can be seen with the rapid growth of hospitality jobs compared to construction. This trend shows how the US economy has progress on the whole even with the major recessions and turmoil of the past 80 years.

The trends of manufacturing and health jobs for the past 80 years. Developed economies throughout the world have seen a shift from labor jobs to serviced based jobs such as health. As seen around the year 2003, healthcare jobs began to grow faster than manufacturing signifying this economic shift. The consistent shift from manufacturing to health jobs shows how the US economy is both constantly changing and quickly adapts to these changes.

The trends of Crude Oil Price vs Saudi Arabian GDP by year. Unlike the Angolan GDP graph, Saudi Arabia's GDP does not follow the price of oil as closely. This recent gap between oil prices and GDP is a signal of Saudi Arabia trying to diversify its economy similar to other developed countries such as the US. The US economy is extremely diversified and therefore, similar to the recent trends in Saudi GDP, does not rely on any specific commodity price.
